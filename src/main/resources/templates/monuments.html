List Of Monumnets

1. Taj Mahal



The Taj Mahal stands as the pinnacle among the nation's monuments. Erected in the 17th century, it serves as the epitome of regal elegance and architectural marvel. Fashioned from marble, this majestic mausoleum dedicated to Mumtaz Mahal is adorned with delicate lattice work and embellished with semi-precious stones sourced from various corners of the globe. Each year, millions of visitors flock to witness the splendor of this monument, and it is highly recommended that you too experience its beauty firsthand.


















2. Agra Fort



Adjacent to the Taj Mahal, the city's primary allure is the Agra Fort, once the epicenter of Mughal rule. While the fort traces its origins back several centuries, it gained prominence as a formidable defense structure following Akbar's reconstruction using sandstone. Shah Jahan further enhanced the fort's grandeur by incorporating numerous marble edifices. Encompassing a sprawling 94 acres, this heritage site boasts notable attractions within its walls, including Khas Mahal, Diwan-i-khas, Nagina Masjid, Diwan-i-aam, Musamman Burj, Shahi Burj, Sheesh Mahal, and others.














3. Fatehpur Sikhri



Situated a short distance from Agra, this expansive city was established in the 16th century by Akbar as a capital. It showcases meticulous architectural splendor, featuring a myriad of structures such as palaces, mosques, and halls. However, due to water scarcity issues, Akbar abandoned the city within a decade. Today, Fatehpur Sikri stands as a prominent attraction, celebrated for its architectural wonders like Jama Masjid, Diwan-i-Aam, Diwan-i-Khas, Buland Darwaza, Mariam uz Zamani, Anup Talao, Ibadat Khana, Pachisi court, Hiran Minar, and others.
















4. Baby Taj or Itimad-ud-daula



The Itimad-Ud-Daulah is the inspiration behind the Taj Mahal, constructed in the 17th century with a combination of sandstone and marble. Dedicated to Nur Jahan's father, this tomb is renowned for its intricate wall designs. Notably, it features an array of semi-precious stones such as jasper, topaz, lapis, onyx, and more. When envisioning the Taj Mahal, Shah Jahan aspired to create a structure surpassing the grandeur of this mausoleum dedicated to his grandfather.
